Psychological realism: when emotions shape the narrative

Psychological realism is a literary approach that seeks to depict the complexity of emotions, thoughts, and motivations of characters in a deep and detailed manner. Unlike traditional realism, which focuses more on the objective description of the external world, psychological realism emphasizes the internal perceptions, inner conflicts, and subjective experiences of the characters.

Lígia is a renowned Brazilian writer often associated with the genre of psychological realism. Her works are characterized by remarkable psychological depth, exploring the nuances of the human mind and the complex emotions of her characters. She delves into the deepest aspects of her characters' psyches, revealing their fears, longings, contradictions, and traumas.

Telles' writing is known for its sensitivity, introspection, and ability to capture the complexities of human relationships. Her characters are portrayed in a multidimensional way, with conflicting motivations and ambiguous feelings. She addresses themes such as loneliness, alienation, madness, love, death, and violence, exploring the impact of these issues on the psychology of her characters.

Furthermore, Lígia Fagundes Telles is also recognized for her skill in creating dense and immersive atmospheres through rich and poetic prose. Her fluid and introspective writing allows readers to emotionally connect with the stories and delve into the depths of the human condition.

In summary, psychological realism is a literary genre that focuses on the exploration of the human mind and emotions, and Lígia Fagundes Telles is a writer who excels in this genre, using her mastery to create complex characters and address profound themes with sensitivity and depth.
